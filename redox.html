Redox Reactions

Classical concept of oxidation/reduction, oxidation number calculations, balancing redox equations, and electrochemical cells.

Concept of Oxidation & Reduction

Oxidation: • Gain of oxygen. • Loss of hydrogen. • **Loss of electrons (De-electronation).** $$Zn \rightarrow Zn^{2+} + 2e^-$$
Reduction: • Loss of oxygen. • Gain of hydrogen. • **Gain of electrons (Electronation).** $$Cu^{2+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu$$

Rules for Oxidation Number

  • Fluorine always has oxidation number -1 in its compounds.
  • Oxygen has -2 (except in peroxides like $H_2O_2$ where it is -1, and $OF_2$ where it is +2).
  • Hydrogen is +1 (except in metallic hydrides like $NaH$ where it is -1).
  • Sum of oxidation numbers in a neutral compound is 0; in an ion, it equals the charge.
