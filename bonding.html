VSEPR Theory Geometries

Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion theory predicts molecular shapes based on minimizing electrostatic repulsions between electron pairs (lone pairs and bond pairs).

Order of Repulsion: $$\text{Lone Pair - Lone Pair} > \text{Lone Pair - Bond Pair} > \text{Bond Pair - Bond Pair}$$

Lone pairs occupy more space, leading to distorted bond angles (e.g., $104.5^\circ$ in water vs tetrahedral $109.5^\circ$).

Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT)

Atomic orbitals of comparable energies combine to form molecular orbitals (bonding and antibonding):

Bond Order calculation: $$\text{Bond Order (B.O.)} = \frac{N_b - N_a}{2}$$ Where $N_b$ is bonding and $N_a$ is antibonding electrons.
Magnetic properties: • Unpaired electrons: **Paramagnetic** • All electrons paired: **Diamagnetic**

Hydrogen Bonding

Attractive force binding hydrogen atom covalently linked to a highly electronegative atom (F, O, N) with another electronegative atom.

1. Intermolecular H-Bonding

Occurs between different molecules of same/different compound (e.g., $H_2O$, $HF$, $NH_3$). Increases boiling points.

2. Intramolecular H-Bonding

Occurs within a single molecule (e.g., o-nitrophenol). Lowers boiling point.
