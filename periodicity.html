Modern Periodic Law

The physical and chemical properties of the elements are periodic functions of their atomic numbers (Moseley, 1913).

Periodic Table Shells: • Periods (horizontal rows): indicate principal quantum shell ($n$). • Groups (vertical columns): contain elements with similar outer electronic configurations.

Atomic Radii Trends

Down a Group: Atomic radius increases. New principal shells are added, increasing the distance between nucleus and valence shell, offsetting the nuclear charge.

Across a Period: Atomic radius decreases. Principal shell remains same while nuclear charge increases, pulling outer electrons closer.

Ionization Enthalpy ($IE$)

The energy required to remove an electron from an isolated gaseous atom in its ground state:

$$X(g) \rightarrow X^+(g) + e^- \quad (\Delta H = IE_1)$$

Across Period: IE increases (atomic size decreases, nuclear pull increases).
Down Group: IE decreases (atomic size increases, shielding increases).
