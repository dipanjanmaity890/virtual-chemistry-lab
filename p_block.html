p-Block Elements

Groups 15, 16, 17 & 18 Properties & Oxoacids

Group 15 Elements (Nitrogen Family)

p-Block Elements

Dinitrogen & Ammonia

• **N₂** is inert at room temperature due to its strong triple bond (N≡N).

• **NH₃ (Ammonia):** Trigonal pyramidal shape with a lone pair. Strong Lewis base.

Manufactured by Haber's Process.

Oxoacids of Phosphorus

• **H₃PO₃ (Orthophosphorous acid):** Dibasic acid (contains 2 P-OH bonds and 1 P-H bond).

• **H₃PO₄ (Orthophosphoric acid):** Tribasic acid (contains 3 P-OH bonds).

P-H bonds are responsible for reducing properties in oxoacids.

Group 16 Elements (Chalcogens)

p-Block Elements

Dioxygen & Ozone

• **O₂** is paramagnetic despite having even electrons (explained by Molecular Orbital Theory).

• **O₃ (Ozone):** Bent structure with resonance stabilizer. Act as strong oxidizing agent.

Oxoacids of Sulfur

• **H₂SO₄ (Sulfuric acid):** Dibasic, strong dehydrating agent (manufactured by Contact Process).

• **H₂S₂O₇ (Oleum):** Formed by absorbing SO₃ in H₂SO₄.

• **H₂S₂O₈ (Marshall's acid):** Contains peroxo linkage (S-O-O-S).

Group 17 Elements (Halogens)

p-Block Elements
  • Acidic strength: HF < HCl < HBr < HI. Bond dissociation enthalpy decreases down the group.
  • Electron Gain Enthalpy: Chlorine has a more negative value than Fluorine due to small size and inter-electronic repulsion in F.

Interhalogen Compounds

Formed when two different halogens react. General formula: XX'_n (n=1,3,5,7).

• **More reactive** than parent halogens (except F₂) because X-X' bonds are polar and weaker than X-X bonds.

• Examples: ClF₃ (T-shaped), IF₇ (Pentagonal bipyramidal).

Group 18 Elements (Noble Gases)

p-Block Elements

Have completely filled valence shells (ns² np⁶), giving them high ionization enthalpies and zero electron gain enthalpies. Inert chemical nature.

  • Helium used in cryogenic applications and filling balloons.
  • Argon provides inert atmospheres in metallurgical processes.

Xenon Compounds

Neil Bartlett prepared the first noble gas compound in 1962. Xenon reacts with fluorine under different temp/pressure to yield:

• XeF₂: Linear geometry (3 lone pairs)
• XeF₄: Square planar (2 lone pairs)
• XeF₆: Distorted octahedral (1 lone pair)

Haber's Process (Ammonia Manufacture)

p-Block Elements

Industrial synthesis of Ammonia gas from nitrogen and hydrogen:

N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g) + Heat
ΔH° = -92.4 kJ/mol (Exothermic)

Le Chatelier's Constraints

According to Le Chatelier's principle, high yield is favoured by:

• High Pressure: ~200 atm (shifts to side with fewer moles).

• Low Temperature: Optimum is ~700 K (exothermic reaction, but low temp slows rate, hence catalyst required).

• Catalyst: Iron oxide with K₂O/Al₂O₃ promoter.
