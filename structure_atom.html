Structure of Atom

Bohr's model, dual nature of matter, Heisenberg uncertainty, orbital shapes, and quantum configuration filling.

Bohr's Model of Hydrogen Atom

  • Electrons revolve around nucleus in fixed circular orbits (energy is quantized).
  • Angular momentum is integral multiple of $h/2\pi$: $$m v r = \frac{n h}{2\pi}$$
  • Energy changes occur only during transition of electron between orbits: $$\Delta E = E_2 - E_1 = h \nu$$
  • Wave number formula: $$\bar{\nu} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right)$$

Quantum Numbers

Four quantum numbers describe the address and spin state of an electron in an atom:

1. Principal ($n$)

Main energy level / shell ($n = 1, 2, 3...$).

2. Azimuthal ($l$)

Subshell shape ($l = 0 \text{ to } n-1$). $s=0, p=1, d=2, f=3$.

3. Magnetic ($m_l$)

Orientation of orbital ($m_l = -l \text{ to } +l$).

4. Spin ($m_s$)

Spin direction of electron ($+1/2$ or $-1/2$).

Rules for Filling Orbitals

1. Aufbau Principle

Orbitals are filled in order of increasing energy ($n+l$ rule).

2. Pauli Exclusion

An orbital can hold max 2 electrons with opposite spins.

3. Hund's Rule

Electron pairing in degenerate orbitals (like p, d) doesn't start until each orbital is singly filled with parallel spin.

de Broglie Relation & Heisenberg Uncertainty

Wave-particle duality applies to all moving subatomic particles:

  • de Broglie Wavelength: Moving matter behaves as waves: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{m v}$$
  • Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: It is impossible to determine position and momentum simultaneously: $$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \ge \frac{h}{4\pi}$$

NCERT Solved Numerical: de Broglie Wavelength

Problem: Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron (mass = $9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$) moving with a velocity of $2.0 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$. ($h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$).

Calculation: $$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 2.0 \times 10^6}$$ $$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{1.822 \times 10^{-24}} = 3.63 \times 10^{-10}\text{ m} = 363\text{ pm}$$
