Isotopes of Hydrogen

Hydrogen has three isotopes with mass numbers 1, 2, and 3:

Protium ($^1H$)

No neutrons.
99.98% abundance.

Deuterium ($^2H$ or $D$)

1 neutron.
Used in heavy water ($D_2O$).

Tritium ($^3H$ or $T$)

2 neutrons.
Radioactive ($\beta^-$ emitter).

Water Hardness

Hardness in water is due to dissolved hydrogen carbonates, sulfates, and chlorides of Calcium and Magnesium.

Temporary Hardness: Due to Calcium/Magnesium bicarbonates. Removed easily by boiling or adding Clark's lime: $$Ca(HCO_3)_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3 \downarrow + H_2O + CO_2$$
Permanent Hardness: Due to Calcium/Magnesium chlorides/sulfates. Removed using Calgon ($Na_6P_6O_{18}$) or ion-exchange zeolites.
