Chemical Reactions and Equations

Types of chemical reactions, balancing equations, exothermic/endothermic reactions, and oxidation-reduction.

What is a Chemical Reaction?

A chemical reaction transforms reactants into new products with different chemical properties. Observable signs include:

  • Change in colour (e.g., blue CuSO4 turns colourless on reduction)
  • Evolution of a gas (e.g., CO2 bubbles from vinegar + baking soda)
  • Formation of a precipitate (white BaSO4 from BaCl2 + Na2SO4)
  • Change in temperature (heat evolution in combustion or absorption in endothermic reactions)
  • Change in state (liquid water to steam on boiling)
Balancing (Law of Conservation of Mass): Equal atoms on both sides.
Unbalanced: H2 + O2 gives H2O | Balanced: 2H2 + O2 gives 2H2O

Types of Chemical Reactions

  • 1. Combination: A + B gives AB. Two or more substances combine to form a single product.
    CaO + H2O gives Ca(OH)2 (heat released, exothermic)
  • 2. Decomposition: AB gives A + B. Single compound breaks into simpler substances. Needs energy (heat/light/electricity).
    2H2O gives 2H2 + O2 (electrolysis) | 2AgCl gives 2Ag + Cl2 (photolysis by sunlight)
  • 3. Displacement: A + BC gives AC + B. More reactive displaces less reactive.
    Fe + CuSO4(aq) gives FeSO4 + Cu (iron is more reactive than copper)
  • 4. Double Displacement: AB + CD gives AD + CB. Ion exchange between two compounds.
    Na2SO4 + BaCl2 gives BaSO4(precipitate) + 2NaCl

Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions

Exothermic (Delta H negative)

Release heat/light to surroundings. Products have lower energy than reactants.

  • Combustion: C + O2 gives CO2 + Heat
  • Respiration: Glucose + O2 gives CO2 + H2O + Energy (slow combustion)
  • Neutralization: HCl + NaOH gives NaCl + H2O + Heat
  • CaO + H2O gives Ca(OH)2 + Heat (slaking of lime)

Endothermic (Delta H positive)

Absorb heat from surroundings. Products have higher energy than reactants.

  • CaCO3 gives CaO + CO2 (limestone decomposition, needs 840C)
  • Photosynthesis: CO2 + H2O gives Glucose + O2 (needs sunlight)
  • Evaporation: absorbs latent heat from surroundings
  • Dissolving NH4Cl in water: cools the water

Oxidation and Reduction

Oxidation: Loss of electrons / Gain of oxygen / Loss of hydrogen / Increase in oxidation number.
Reduction: Gain of electrons / Loss of oxygen / Gain of hydrogen / Decrease in oxidation number.
Oxidising Agent: Causes oxidation of others, itself gets reduced.
Reducing Agent: Causes reduction of others, itself gets oxidised.

CuO + H2 gives Cu + H2O
H2 (loses nothing, gains O) is oxidised to H2O. CuO (loses O) is reduced to Cu.
H2 = reducing agent | CuO = oxidising agent

Zn + CuSO4 gives ZnSO4 + Cu
Zn loses 2 electrons (oxidised). Cu2+ gains 2 electrons (reduced).
