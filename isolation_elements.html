Occurrence of Metals

Minerals: Naturally occurring chemical substances in the earth's crust obtainable by mining.

Ores: Minerals from which a metal can be extracted **conveniently and economically**.

"All ores are minerals, but all minerals are not ores."

Major Ores of CBSE syllabus

• Iron: Haematite (Fe₂O₃), Magnetite (Fe₃O₄)

• Copper: Copper pyrites (CuFeS₂), Malachite [CuCO₃·Cu(OH)₂]

• Zinc: Zinc blende (ZnS), Calamine (ZnCO₃)

Hydraulic Washing

Based on differences in **specific gravity** of ore and gangue. Light gangue washed away by water stream (e.g. oxide ores of Fe/Sn).

Magnetic Separation

Based on magnetic properties. Ground ore passes over magnetic roller (e.g. separating magnetic Fe ores from non-magnetic gangue).

Froth Flotation

Separates **sulfide ores** from gangue. Hydrophobic ore attaches to pine oil-air bubbles and rises; hydrophilic gangue sinks in water.

Calcination vs Roasting

Isolation of Elements

Concentrated ore is converted to metal oxide for easier reduction.

Calcination

Heating the ore **in the absence / limited supply of air** below its melting point.

• Used for **carbonate and hydrated oxide ores**.

• Drives off moisture and volatile organics.

ZnCO₃ + Heat ➔ ZnO + CO₂

Roasting

Heating the ore **in a regular supply of air** at a temperature below its melting point.

• Used for **sulfide ores**.

• Impurities like S, As, Sb escape as volatile oxides.

2ZnS + 3O₂ + Heat ➔ 2ZnO + 2SO₂

Ellingham Diagram

Isolation of Elements

A plot of **standard Gibbs free energy change (ΔG°)** for the formation of oxides of elements against temperature.

  • Helps choose the right reducing agent for a metal oxide.
  • A metal can reduce oxides of other metals that lie **above** it in the Ellingham diagram (since ΔG° of net reaction will be negative).

Thermodynamics Rule

ΔG° = ΔH° - TΔS°

If slope of ΔG° line is positive, entropy decreases during oxide formation. Coupling reactions allows reduction calculations.

Extraction of Iron (Blast Furnace)

Isolation of Elements

Iron oxide ore (Hematite) is reduced in a Blast Furnace using coke (C) and limestone (CaCO₃).

  • Reduction zone (500-800 K): Fe₂O₃ is reduced by CO: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO ➔ 2Fe + 3CO₂.
  • Slag formation zone (900-1500 K): Limestone decomposes: CaCO₃ ➔ CaO + CO₂. CaO reacts with SiO₂ gangue: CaO + SiO₂ ➔ CaSiO₃ (slag).

Types of Iron

• Pig Iron: Direct blast furnace product. Contains ~4% Carbon. Hard and brittle.

• Cast Iron: Made by melting pig iron with scrap. Contains ~3% Carbon.

• Wrought Iron: Purest commercial form of iron (<0.5% carbon). Malleable.

Refining / Purification

Isolation of Elements

Electrolytic Refining

Anode is impure metal; cathode is pure sheet. Under potential, pure metal deposits on cathode (e.g. Copper, Zinc purification).

Zone Refining

Based on principle that impurities are more soluble in molten state. Moving heater creates ultra-pure silicon, germanium for semiconductors.

Vapour Phase Refining

Metal converted to volatile compound, then decomposed (e.g. **Mond Process** for Nickel; **van Arkel** for Zirconium/Titanium).
