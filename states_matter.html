States of Matter

Intermolecular forces, gaseous laws, Boyle's & Charles's rules, and ideal gas behavior.

Intermolecular Forces

  • Dispersion / London Forces: Weak attractive forces between temporary dipoles in non-polar molecules (e.g., Helium, Nitrogen).
  • Dipole-Dipole Forces: Occur between molecules having permanent dipoles (e.g., $HCl$).
  • Dipole-Induced Dipole: A polar molecule induces a dipole in a non-polar molecule nearby.

The Gas Laws

Boyle's Law

At constant temperature, pressure of a fixed mass of gas is inversely proportional to its volume:

$$P \propto \frac{1}{V} \implies P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2$$

Charles's Law

At constant pressure, volume of a fixed mass of gas is directly proportional to absolute temperature:

$$V \propto T \implies \frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2}$$

Ideal Gas Equation

Combining Boyle's, Charles's, and Avogadro's laws gives the Ideal Gas law:

$$P V = n R T$$

Gas Constant ($R$): • $R = 8.314 \text{ J mol}^{-1} \text{ K}^{-1}$ • $R = 0.0821 \text{ L atm mol}^{-1} \text{ K}^{-1}$
