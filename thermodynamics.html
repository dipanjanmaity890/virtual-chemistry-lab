First Law of Thermodynamics

Energy of an isolated system is constant. Mathematically:

$$\Delta U = q + w$$

Where $\Delta U$ is change in internal energy, $q$ is heat added, and $w$ is work done on system.

Enthalpy ($H$): $$H = U + PV \implies \Delta H = \Delta U + P\Delta V$$ • $\Delta H < 0$: **Exothermic Reaction**
• $\Delta H > 0$: **Endothermic Reaction**

Hess's Law

If a reaction takes place in several steps then its standard reaction enthalpy is the sum of standard enthalpies of the intermediate steps.

Hess's Equation: $$\Delta H_{\text{target}} = \Delta H_1 + \Delta H_2 + \Delta H_3 + ...$$ This shows enthalpy is a **state function** independent of the pathway.

Gibbs Energy and Spontaneity

Gibbs energy ($G$) determines thermodynamic spontaneity at constant temperature and pressure:

$$\Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S$$
