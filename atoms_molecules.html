Atoms and Molecules

Dalton's atomic theory, laws of chemical combination, mole concept, and molecular mass calculations.

Dalton's Atomic Theory

  • All matter is made of very small particles called atoms (from Greek "atomos" meaning indivisible).
  • Atoms are indivisible and indestructible (modified by discovery of sub-atomic particles).
  • All atoms of a given element are identical in mass and chemical properties.
  • Atoms of different elements have different masses and chemical properties.
  • Atoms combine in simple whole number ratios to form compounds.
  • In a chemical reaction, atoms are only rearranged, not created or destroyed.

Laws of Chemical Combination

Law of Conservation of Mass (Lavoisier, 1789)

Total mass of reactants = Total mass of products. Matter is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.

Example: 6 g C + 16 g O2 gives 22 g CO2. Total = 22 g on both sides.

Law of Constant Proportions (Proust, 1799)

A pure compound always contains the same elements in the same definite ratio by mass, regardless of its source or method of preparation.

H2O always has H:O = 1:8 by mass (from Nile or Ganga or lab-prepared water).

Atomic Mass and Molecular Mass

Atomic mass is relative, measured in Atomic Mass Units (u) where 1 u = 1/12 the mass of a Carbon-12 atom.

  • H=1u, O=16u, N=14u, C=12u, S=32u, Na=23u, Cl=35.5u, Ca=40u, Fe=56u, Cu=63.5u
  • Molecular Mass = Sum of atomic masses of all atoms in the molecule (including multiplicity).
  • H2O: 2(1)+16 = 18 u | H2SO4: 2(1)+32+4(16) = 98 u
  • NaCl: 23+35.5 = 58.5 u | CaCO3: 40+12+3(16) = 100 u

The Mole Concept

The mole is the SI unit for amount of substance. 1 mole of any substance contains exactly 6.022 x 10^23 particles (Avogadro's Number, NA).

Moles = Given mass (g) / Molar mass (g/mol)
Number of particles = Moles x Avogadro's Number (6.022 x 10^23)

1 mole H2O = 18 g = 6.022 x 10^23 water molecules
1 mole NaCl = 58.5 g = 6.022 x 10^23 formula units

Why is the mole useful? Atoms are too small to count individually. The mole links atomic mass (in u) to laboratory masses (in grams).

Criss-Cross Valency Rules

Valency is the combining capacity of an atom. We write chemical formulas by crossing valencies:

Aluminium Oxide: * Symbol: $Al$ and $O$ * Valency: $+3$ and $-2$ * Cross-over: **$Al_2O_3$**
Calcium Chloride: * Symbol: $Ca$ and $Cl$ * Valency: $+2$ and $-1$ * Cross-over: **$CaCl_2$**

NCERT Solved Numerical: Molecular Mass

Formula: Sum of atomic masses of all atoms present $$\text{Mass of } A_xB_y = x \times \text{Mass}(A) + y \times \text{Mass}(B)$$

Problem: Calculate the molecular mass of Nitric Acid ($HNO_3$). (Atomic masses: $H=1$, $N=14$, $O=16$).

Step-by-step Calculation: $$\text{Mass of } H = 1 \times 1\text{ u}$$ $$\text{Mass of } N = 1 \times 14\text{ u} = 14\text{ u}$$ $$\text{Mass of } O = 3 \times 16\text{ u} = 48\text{ u}$$ $$\text{Molecular Mass} = 1 + 14 + 48 = 63\text{ u}$$
